        <p><span style="font-size: 9pt; font-family: calibri, sans-serif;">Service: CRISIL Mutual Fund Ranking Tracker</span></p>
        <p><strong><span style="font-size: 11pt; font-family: calibri, sans-serif;">1. Scope of Service</span></strong></p>
        <p style="text-align: justify;"><span style="font-size: 9pt; font-family: calibri, sans-serif;">The Service comprises the preparation and delivery of the CRISIL Mutual Fund Ranking Tracker Report in accordance with the scope, particulars, frequency and other requirements specified in Annexure II (each, an <strong><em>MFRT Report</em></strong>).</span></p>
        <p><span style="font-size: 11pt;"><strong><span style="font-family: calibri, sans-serif;">2. Process flow</span></strong></span></p>
        <p><span style="font-size: 9pt; font-family: calibri, sans-serif;">2.1 CRISIL Mutual Fund Ranking Tracker Report:</span></p>
        <p style="text-align: justify;"><span style="font-size: 9pt; font-family: calibri, sans-serif;">CRISIL Mutual Fund Ranking Tracker is the relative performance ranking of mutual fund schemes within the peer group. The basic eligibility criteria for inclusion in the ranking universe are two years NAV history (6 months for Liquid, Ultra Short Term Debt, Index and Debt-Short funds) and assets under management in excess of category cut-off limits. The ranking is done based on the following parameters:</span></p>
        <p><span style="font-size: 9pt; font-family: calibri, sans-serif;">(a)<span style="font-style: normal; font-variant: normal; font-weight: normal; font-stretch: normal; line-height: normal;"> </span>Superior Return Score</span></p>
        <p style="text-align: justify;"><span style="font-size: 9pt; font-family: calibri, sans-serif;">The Superior Return Score (SRS) is the relative measure of the return and the risk for the schemes compared to their peer group. It is computed for Equity Oriented Categories, Equity Linked Saving Schemes (ELSS), Debt, Balanced, Monthly Income Plan (Aggressive &amp; Conservative) and Gilt categories for a two - year period. The weights for the four six-month periods starting from the latest are 32.5%. 27.5%, 22.5% and 17.5%, respectively.</span></p>
        <p><span style="font-size: 9pt; font-family: calibri, sans-serif;">(b)<span style="font-style: normal; font-variant: normal; font-weight: normal; font-stretch: normal; line-height: normal;"> </span>Mean Return and Volatility</span></p>
        <p style="text-align: justify;"><span style="font-size: 9pt; font-family: calibri, sans-serif;">Mean Return and Volatility are considered separately in case of Liquid, Ultra Short Term Debt and Debt-Short term schemes for a six - month period. Mean return is average of daily return on fund NAV for the latest six - month period and the Volatility is the standard deviation of these returns.</span><span style="font-size: 9pt; font-family: calibri, sans-serif;">The weightages for the two quarterly periods starting from the latest are 60% and 40% respectively.</span></p>
        <h3><span style="font-size: 11pt; font-family: calibri, sans-serif;">(c)<span style="font-style: normal; font-variant: normal; font-weight: normal; font-stretch: normal; line-height: normal;"> </span>Portfolio Concentration Analysis</span></h3>
        <p style="text-align: justify;"><span style="font-size: 9pt; font-family: calibri, sans-serif;">Concentration measures the risk arising out of improper diversification. Diversity Score is used as the parameter to measure Industry concentration and Company concentration for equity securities. In case of Debt papers, the industry concentration is analyzed only for any / over exposure in sensitive sectors which is arrived at based on CRISIL's assessment of the prospects for various sectors </span><span style="font-size: 9pt; font-family: calibri, sans-serif;">and Company concentration will have an individual issuer specific limit of 10.0%</span></p>
        <h3 style="text-align: justify;"><span style="font-size: 11pt; font-family: calibri, sans-serif;">(d)<span style="font-style: normal; font-variant: normal; font-weight: normal; font-stretch: normal; line-height: normal;"> </span>Liquidity Analysis</span></h3>
        <p style="text-align: justify;"><span style="font-size: 9pt; font-family: calibri, sans-serif;">It measures the ease with which the portfolio can be liquidated. In case of Equities, liquidity is calculated by taking the average of last 1, 2 or 3 months impact cost published by stock exchanges. Gilt liquidity is measured by analysing the market turnover, days traded and size of trade in any security for a one, two or three months period for that security. Corporate Debt Liquidity is computed by classifying each security into 3 categories - Liquid, Semi Liquid and Illiquid and then evaluating a fund's exposure to each such category.</span></p>
        <h3><span style="font-size: 11pt; font-family: calibri, sans-serif;">(e)<span style="font-style: normal; font-variant: normal; font-weight: normal; font-stretch: normal; line-height: normal;"> </span>Asset Quality</span></h3>
        <p style="text-align: justify;"><span style="font-size: 9pt; font-family: calibri, sans-serif;">Asset Quality measures the probability of default by the issuer of a debt security to honour the debt obligation in time. CRISIL penalizes lower rated securities to a higher level in its analysis.</span></p>
        <h3><span style="font-size: 11pt; font-family: calibri, sans-serif;">(f)<span style="font-style: normal; font-variant: normal; font-weight: normal; font-stretch: normal; line-height: normal;"> </span>Modified Duration /Average Maturity</span></h3>
        <p style="text-align: justify;"><span style="font-size: 9pt; font-family: calibri, sans-serif;">Modified duration /Average Maturity are considered across all debt categories except liquid to capture the interest rate risk of the portfolio. Lower the value better it is.</span></p>
        <h3><span style="font-size: 11pt; font-family: calibri, sans-serif;">(g)<span style="font-style: normal; font-variant: normal; font-weight: normal; font-stretch: normal; line-height: normal;"> </span>Downside Risk Probability (DRP)</span></h3>
        <p style="text-align: justify;"><span style="font-size: 9pt; font-family: calibri, sans-serif;">DRP measures the probability of the investment getting lower returns than short tenor risk free securities. It measured by assessing the number of times a fund's return falls below the risk free return over the period of analysis. The risk free return is taken as the 91-day T-Bill yield over the period.</span></p>
        <h3 style="text-align: justify;"><span style="font-size: 11pt; font-family: calibri, sans-serif;">(h)<span style="font-style: normal; font-variant: normal; font-weight: normal; font-stretch: normal; line-height: normal;"> </span>Asset Size</span></h3>
        <p style="text-align: justify;"><span style="font-size: 9pt; font-family: calibri, sans-serif;">It is considered only for Ultra Short Term Debt and Liquid categories to take into account the effect of large fund flows on the fund's performance and ability of the funds to manage such flows optimally. Higher the asset size better it is.</span></p>
        <h3><span style="font-size: 11pt; font-family: calibri, sans-serif;">(i)<span style="font-style: normal; font-variant: normal; font-weight: normal; font-stretch: normal; line-height: normal;"> </span>Tracking Error</span></h3>
        <p style="text-align: justify;"><span style="font-size: 9pt; font-family: calibri, sans-serif;">This is used only for Index funds. The tracking error is an estimation of the variability in a fund's performance vis-&agrave;-vis the index that it tracks. Lower the tracking error better it is.</span></p>
